Q: What is the difference between document, manual, guide, wiki?

A: The terms document, manual, guide, and wiki refer to different types of written materials, each serving distinct purposes and structures. Here's a breakdown of each term:

1. **Document**:
   - A document is a broad term that refers to any written or digital piece of information that conveys specific content. Documents can come in various formats, including reports, letters, forms, or memos, and they may serve different purposes, such as recording information or communicating messages. Examples include PDFs, Word files, and Google Docs.

2. **Manual**:
   - A manual is a type of document that provides detailed instructions or guidelines on how to perform a specific task, use a product, or operate a system. Manuals are usually structured in a step-by-step format and cover various aspects such as setup, troubleshooting, and maintenance. Examples include user manuals, service manuals, and equipment manuals.

3. **Guide**:
   - A guide is a document designed to offer advice, instructions, or recommendations on a particular subject or process. While guides may provide some detailed instructions, they are often less comprehensive than manuals and can include broader information, tips, and best practices. For example, a travel guide offers insights into destinations, accommodations, and activities.

4. **Wiki**:
   - A wiki is a collaborative platform that allows multiple users to create, edit, and organize content collectively. A wiki often contains articles, guidelines, and documents that are easily accessible and can be modified by anyone with the appropriate permissions. Wikipedia is a well-known example of a wiki. Wikis are useful for creating a shared knowledge base or documentation that evolves over time.

In summary, documents are the broadest category and can refer to any written content, while manuals and guides have specific instructional purposes. A wiki is a collaborative platform for creating and managing content collectively.
